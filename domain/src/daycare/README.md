# `domain::daycare`

`domain::daycare` is the domain crate's model for daytime pet-resort care. It owns daycare concepts that should not be flattened into provider payloads, database codes, or front-desk notes: service variants and care modes, attendance rules, prepaid package policy, staff-to-pet coverage, group-play eligibility, playgroup assignment, incident restrictions, front-desk check-in lanes, and package-opportunity review.

Start at [`mod.rs`](./mod.rs). It declares the module surface, defines shared validated scalars such as [`domain::daycare::PackageVisits`](./mod.rs), [`StaffCount`](./mod.rs), and [`PetCount`](./mod.rs), maps [`domain::daycare::ServiceVariant`](./mod.rs) values to [`CareMode`](./mod.rs), and collects policy values into [`domain::daycare::Contract`](./mod.rs). `Contract::standard_petsuites` is a fixture-like standard contract used by service-contract storage and tests; it is not a complete catalog of every daycare product.

## Module navigation

- [`mod.rs`](./mod.rs) defines cross-cutting daycare contract vocabulary: [`ServiceVariant`](./mod.rs), [`CareMode`](./mod.rs), [`AttendancePolicy`](./mod.rs), [`PackagePolicy`](./mod.rs), [`StaffPetRatio`](./mod.rs), [`GroupAssignmentRule`](./mod.rs), [`EligibilityRequirement`](./mod.rs), and [`Contract`](./mod.rs). `Contract::requires_staff_review_before_group_play` keeps staff-review expectations attached to the group-assignment and eligibility contract rather than scattering them through check-in code.
- [`attendance.rs`](./attendance.rs) defines recurring daycare attendance materialization: [`domain::daycare::attendance::DateRange`](./attendance.rs), [`Days`](./attendance.rs), [`Recurrence`](./attendance.rs), and [`Materializer`](./attendance.rs). `Materializer::materialize` expands only the requested weekdays inside the date range and skips explicit exception dates.
- [`coverage.rs`](./coverage.rs) defines staffing coverage review: [`domain::daycare::coverage::RosterSnapshot`](./coverage.rs), [`Decision`](./coverage.rs), [`InsufficiencyReason`](./coverage.rs), and [`Policy`](./coverage.rs). `Policy::evaluate` compares checked-in pets against the contract's [`StaffPetRatio`](./mod.rs) and returns manager-review insufficiency when the ratio is exceeded.
- [`eligibility.rs`](./eligibility.rs) defines group-play eligibility evidence and decisions: [`domain::daycare::eligibility::Evidence`](./eligibility.rs), [`TemperamentAssessmentFreshness`](./eligibility.rs), [`VaccineReadiness`](./eligibility.rs), [`GroupPlayDecision`](./eligibility.rs), [`EligibleBasis`](./eligibility.rs), [`ReviewReason`](./eligibility.rs), [`DenialReason`](./eligibility.rs), and [`GroupPlayPolicy`](./eligibility.rs). `GroupPlayPolicy::evaluate` gates dog group play on species/care mode, temperament assessment freshness, vaccine proof, spay/neuter status, incident restrictions, and staff coverage.
- [`assignment.rs`](./assignment.rs) defines playgroup assignment: [`domain::daycare::assignment::playgroup_id::Id`](./assignment.rs) / [`PlaygroupId`](./assignment.rs), [`Request`](./assignment.rs), [`Decision`](./assignment.rs), [`WaitlistReason`](./assignment.rs), [`BlockReason`](./assignment.rs), and [`Service`](./assignment.rs). `Service::assign` assigns only when group-play eligibility is clear and coverage is sufficient; otherwise it returns a waitlist or behavior-review block.
- [`incident.rs`](./incident.rs) defines daycare-specific incident policy and restrictions: [`domain::daycare::incident::Policy`](./incident.rs), [`Severity`](./incident.rs), [`Restriction`](./incident.rs), [`Disposition`](./incident.rs), and [`Classifier`](./incident.rs). `Classifier::classify` can translate a daycare incident severity into a group-play suspension and the required `domain::policy::ReviewGate`.
- [`front_desk.rs`](./front_desk.rs) defines check-in throughput and queue lanes: [`domain::daycare::front_desk::ReadinessContext`](./front_desk.rs), [`EligibilityReadiness`](./front_desk.rs), [`CareReadiness`](./front_desk.rs), [`PackageReadiness`](./front_desk.rs), [`CustomerMessageReadiness`](./front_desk.rs), [`ReadinessDecision`](./front_desk.rs), [`QueueLane`](./front_desk.rs), [`QueueTicket`](./front_desk.rs), and [`ThroughputPolicy`](./front_desk.rs). `ThroughputPolicy::evaluate` turns eligibility, coverage, care, package, and customer-message readiness into fast-lane, collection, care-team, manager-review, or policy-block lanes.
- [`package_opportunity.rs`](./package_opportunity.rs) defines package-opportunity review: [`domain::daycare::package_opportunity::Evidence`](./package_opportunity.rs), [`AttendanceVisitCount`](./package_opportunity.rs), [`CareEligibility`](./package_opportunity.rs), [`PackageState`](./package_opportunity.rs), [`PaymentState`](./package_opportunity.rs), [`Decision`](./package_opportunity.rs), [`OpportunityScore`](./package_opportunity.rs), [`SuppressionReason`](./package_opportunity.rs), [`NoOpportunityReason`](./package_opportunity.rs), and [`Policy`](./package_opportunity.rs). `Policy::classify` can recommend staff review for frequent pay-per-visit attendance, but suppresses recommendations behind safety/care or billing review gates.

## Daycare workflow surface

The labor-cost-reduction surface is exception triage around high-volume daily attendance. The module gives front-desk staff, care-team staff, and managers typed answers to questions that otherwise become manual policy lookups or Slack-style handoffs.

1. A location's daycare contract starts as [`domain::daycare::Contract`](./mod.rs): attendance policy, package policy, staffing ratio, group-assignment rule, incident policy, and eligibility requirements. This lets storage and app workflows share one semantic contract instead of duplicating policy in provider fields.
2. Recurring attendance is represented by [`domain::daycare::attendance::Recurrence`](./attendance.rs). [`Materializer`](./attendance.rs) produces concrete dates while preserving explicit exception dates, which reduces manual calendar cleanup for regular daycare packages.
3. Coverage review combines [`domain::daycare::coverage::RosterSnapshot`](./coverage.rs) with [`StaffPetRatio`](./mod.rs). [`coverage::Policy`](./coverage.rs) names a ratio-exceeded manager-review gate instead of leaving staff to infer whether a room or group is over capacity.
4. Group-play eligibility uses [`domain::daycare::eligibility::Evidence`](./eligibility.rs) gathered from pet/service/incident/staffing state. [`GroupPlayPolicy`](./eligibility.rs) produces eligible, staff-review, ineligible, or temporarily suspended outcomes with explicit review reasons and gates.
5. Playgroup assignment is a separate step in [`assignment.rs`](./assignment.rs). [`domain::daycare::assignment::Service`](./assignment.rs) only assigns cleared pets into a [`PlaygroupId`](./assignment.rs); insufficient coverage becomes waitlist, and uncleared eligibility becomes a behavior-review block.
6. Incidents flow through [`domain::daycare::incident::Classifier`](./incident.rs). A `SuspendGroupPlay` severity creates a [`Restriction::SuspendedPendingManagerReview`](./incident.rs), which [`GroupPlayPolicy`](./eligibility.rs) reads before allowing future group-play decisions.
7. Front-desk check-in uses [`domain::daycare::front_desk::ReadinessContext`](./front_desk.rs) and [`ThroughputPolicy`](./front_desk.rs) to route pets into fast-lane, collection, care-team review, manager review, or policy-block lanes. The domain decision is only routing/readiness; it does not send customer messages or mutate a reservation.
8. Package opportunities use [`domain::daycare::package_opportunity::Policy`](./package_opportunity.rs) to surface frequent pay-per-visit usage for staff review while suppressing recommendations when safety/care review or billing review is unresolved.

## Cross-crate relationships

- The domain crate exposes this module from [`domain/src/lib.rs`](../lib.rs) as `pub mod daycare`.
- `domain::operations::ServiceOffering::Daycare` links customer-facing service offerings to [`domain::operations::DaycareFormat`](../operations.rs) and [`DaycareEligibilityRule`](../operations.rs) in [`domain/src/operations.rs`](../operations.rs). Those operations values mirror service-catalog shape; they are not replacements for the richer `domain::daycare` policy modules.
- `domain::operations::service_core::ServiceContracts` includes `daycare: domain::daycare::Contract` in [`domain/src/operations.rs`](../operations.rs), alongside boarding, grooming, training, and retail contracts.
- Pet and reservation identity enter daycare through `domain::entities::PetId`, `CustomerId`, and `reservation::Id` in [`domain/src/entities.rs`](../entities.rs), while `domain::pet::Name` in [`domain/src/pet.rs`](../pet.rs) appears in app-level daily-update messaging. Keep identity/name values from those modules rather than inventing daycare-local pet primitives.
- Staff work is represented outside this module by [`domain::staff::Task`](../staff.rs) and task kinds such as `PlaygroupAssessment`, `CheckInPrep`, `DailyUpdateDraft`, `DocumentReview`, and `IncidentFollowUp` in [`domain/src/staff.rs`](../staff.rs). `domain::daycare` produces review gates and readiness decisions that can feed those staff tasks; it does not assign employees or complete tasks itself.
- Shared incident vocabulary such as [`domain::incident::Category`](../incident.rs), [`Severity`](../incident.rs), [`Status`](../incident.rs), and [`Summary`](../incident.rs) lives in [`domain/src/incident.rs`](../incident.rs). `domain::daycare::incident` is the daycare-specific policy layer that turns a daycare incident severity into group-play restriction and review gates.
- App daily-update flows in [`app/src/daily_update.rs`](../../../app/src/daily_update.rs) draft customer-safe care updates from staff notes and always require review before sending. Daycare front-desk and incident decisions can explain why a note/update needs behavior, medical, manager, or customer-message approval, but the app workflow owns the message draft/send stub.
- App manager-briefing flows in [`app/src/manager_daily_brief.rs`](../../../app/src/manager_daily_brief.rs) aggregate source-grounded actions such as demand-versus-staffing review, checkout exception audit, and source-data-quality triage. Daycare coverage, front-desk lanes, package opportunities, and incident gates are the kind of typed source facts/actions that can reduce morning dashboard reconciliation and manager exception scans.
- `storage::service_line::daycare` persists migrated daycare contracts and service-offering codes in [`storage/src/service_line/daycare.rs`](../../../storage/src/service_line/daycare.rs). `ContractRecord` wraps [`domain::daycare::Contract`](./mod.rs); `FormatCode` and `EligibilityRuleCode` convert to and from `domain::operations` daycare catalog values.
- `storage::operations::ServiceOfferingRecord` stores daycare service offerings as `daycare_format` and `daycare_eligibility_rules` in [`storage/src/operations.rs`](../../../storage/src/operations.rs), with shape checks that keep daycare fields off boarding/grooming/training/retail variants.
- Contract round-trip coverage exists in [`storage/tests/core_service_contract_storage.rs`](../../../storage/tests/core_service_contract_storage.rs), and service-offering shape coverage exists in [`storage/tests/operations_storage_contracts.rs`](../../../storage/tests/operations_storage_contracts.rs). Domain behavior coverage for daycare contracts, service variants, eligibility, coverage, assignment, incidents, attendance, package opportunities, and front-desk lanes lives in [`domain/tests/petsuites_core_service_contracts.rs`](../../tests/petsuites_core_service_contracts.rs).
- Source/provider provenance lives in [`domain::source`](../source.rs), including `source::System::Gingr`, `CapacityInventory`, and `LaborScheduling`. Gingr reservation/customer endpoint wrappers are provider-boundary code in [`integrations/gingr/src/endpoint/reservations.rs`](../../../integrations/gingr/src/endpoint/reservations.rs), [`integrations/gingr/src/endpoint/owners_animals.rs`](../../../integrations/gingr/src/endpoint/owners_animals.rs), [`integrations/gingr/src/response.rs`](../../../integrations/gingr/src/response.rs), and [`integrations/gingr/src/webhook.rs`](../../../integrations/gingr/src/webhook.rs); no Gingr daycare-specific DTO or mapper is currently present under [`integrations/gingr/src`](../../../integrations/gingr/src).

## Maintainer notes

- Add new daycare policy where the domain concept owns the decision: recurrence in [`attendance.rs`](./attendance.rs), staffing ratios in [`coverage.rs`](./coverage.rs), group-play evidence in [`eligibility.rs`](./eligibility.rs), playgroup placement in [`assignment.rs`](./assignment.rs), incident restrictions in [`incident.rs`](./incident.rs), check-in routing in [`front_desk.rs`](./front_desk.rs), and package review in [`package_opportunity.rs`](./package_opportunity.rs).
- Preserve module-qualified names in prose and code. `domain::daycare::coverage::Decision`, `domain::daycare::eligibility::GroupPlayDecision`, `domain::daycare::assignment::Decision`, `domain::daycare::front_desk::ReadinessDecision`, and `domain::daycare::package_opportunity::Decision` are intentionally different decisions even though several use the leaf name `Decision`.
- Keep provider-specific reservation, owner/animal, webhook, and raw response details in `integrations/gingr`. Promote only validated, semantically named values into `domain::daycare` and attach provider evidence through `domain::source::RecordRef` rather than raw provider payloads.
- Keep application workflows thin: app code can translate daycare decisions into staff tasks, manager-brief actions, approval gates, queue lanes, or customer-message review requirements, but the policy and invariants should remain in `domain::daycare`.
