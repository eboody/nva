# Labor-cost with human-review crosswalk

Purpose: help product, operations, IT, and compliance readers reduce front-desk and manager labor cost without inventing unsafe automation authority. This page translates the labor-cost loops in `docs/design/labor-cost-reduction-crosswalk.md` and the safety evidence in `docs/safety/source-evidence-map.md` into a business-facing checklist: what agents may read, what they may draft or recommend, who reviews the work, what they never directly do, what outcome is captured, and why the labor burden falls.

Use this as an entrypoint for roadmap and prioritization conversations. It is not a production-permission approval and does not add authority beyond the cited app/domain/storage contracts.

## Crosswalk

## Safe savings, not unsafe automation

The savings model is intentionally conservative: agents reduce data-gathering, retyping, dashboard reconciliation, repetitive draft-writing, and source-lookup labor. The app/domain/storage contracts still own facts, policy context, allowed and blocked actions, validation, review gates, provenance, and outcome capture.

That means customer-impacting, medical, vaccine, behavior/safety, payment, refund, discount, provider/PMS mutation, schedule/payroll, personnel, and policy decisions remain human-authorized or blocked unless a later source-backed app contract explicitly approves a narrow write path. A model may prepare a booking triage packet, draft a customer reply, rank checkout exceptions, or summarize a stale-vaccine evidence problem; it does not become the actor that confirms the booking, diagnoses the pet, sends the message, charges/refunds money, changes the provider record, edits staffing, or makes policy exceptions.

Labor-cost claims should follow the same boundary. It is fair to say a workflow is designed to reduce manual reconciliation or drafting when it creates a ranked queue, reviewed draft, or source-backed summary. It is not fair to claim ROI or payroll reduction unless outcome records show before/actual minutes, reviewer disposition, source refs, and caveats such as wrong-source or missing-source findings.

## How this ties back to the source evidence

- `docs/design/labor-cost-reduction-crosswalk.md` defines the operating thesis: labor cost falls when managers and front-desk teams stop rediscovering source facts, reconciling dashboards by hand, rewriting repetitive drafts, and reworking ambiguous records while deterministic app contracts retain authority.
- `docs/safety/source-evidence-map.md` identifies the citation surfaces for review-gated agent specs, prompt packets, booking triage, Manager Daily Brief, tool ports, policy review gates, source provenance, storage outcome records, and blocked live side effects.
- The strongest current support is Manager Daily Brief and related outcome capture. Other rows should be treated as roadmap/prioritization targets unless the cited source/app contract already exists and has review-gated outcome records.
- Any future update should keep the same pattern: source facts read, draft/recommendation produced, human reviewer named, blocked actions explicit, outcome captured, and labor-cost mechanism stated without unsupported ROI numbers.

## Glossary

| Term | Plain-English meaning for cross-functional readers |
| --- | --- |
| [Agent](../glossary-workflow-state-terms.md#agent-spec) | A model-driven helper that consumes an app-owned context packet and returns a draft, summary, classification, or recommendation for review. It is not an independent operator of provider, payment, schedule, or messaging systems. |
| [Workflow packet / app-owned context packet](../glossary-workflow-state-terms.md#workflow-packet) | The structured evidence bundle the app builds before asking an agent for help: workflow name, source event, typed input, policies, source refs, allowed actions, blocked actions, and expected output schema. |
| [Review gate](../glossary-workflow-state-terms.md#review-gate) | A named human approval point such as manager approval, medical document review, behavior review, customer message approval, or refund/deposit exception review. |
| [Source ref / provenance](../glossary-source-data-terms.md#domainsourceprovenance-and-domainsourcerecordref-as-data-evidence) | A trace back to where a fact came from: source system, record identity, extraction batch/time, request scope, schema version, payload hash, or raw payload reference when available. |
| [Draft-only](../glossary-workflow-state-terms.md#draft) | The agent may propose words, tasks, rankings, or rationale, but the app and reviewer decide whether anything becomes staff-visible, customer-facing, or operationally executed. |
| Provider/PMS mutation | A live write to systems such as reservation, customer, pet, stay, room, schedule, or operational provider records. This remains blocked or separately approved; reading evidence is not the same as writing back. |
| [Blocked action](../glossary-workflow-state-terms.md#blocked-action) | A live action the app/agent workflow must not perform directly, even when it can prepare evidence or a draft for the right human/system-of-record process. |
| [Outcome capture](../glossary-workflow-state-terms.md#outcome-capture) | The durable record of what staff did with a recommendation: approved, edited, sent by human, completed, deferred, wrong source, suppressed, actual minutes, reviewer, source refs, and caveats. |
| Labor-cost mechanism | The specific manual work being reduced, such as dashboard reconciliation, repeated source lookup, retyping, draft writing, exception prioritization, or report assembly. This is different from an unsupported ROI claim. |
| Safe automation | Automation that ranks, drafts, validates, summarizes, and records outcomes while preserving human authority over customer-impacting, medical/safety, payment, provider-mutation, scheduling, and policy decisions. |
