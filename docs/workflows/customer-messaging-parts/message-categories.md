# Message categories

Purpose: define the source-backed customer-message category taxonomy for the Customer Messaging Agent. This is a workflow/specification artifact only. It does not approve production sends, provider mutations, payment actions, booking confirmations, medical/vaccine decisions, incident notices, review solicitations, or customer-facing policy copy.

Status: draft taxonomy for downstream Customer Messaging Agent definition work. Categories are derived from `docs/workflows/customer-messaging-parts/inputs.md` and the related workflow input packets for inquiry intake, booking triage, vaccine documents, staff operations, daily care updates, incidents, and payments. No approved template catalog, consent/opt-out model, quiet-hours policy, or WhatsApp support exists in the current repo sources.

Global defaults:

- Current channels: email, SMS, and portal. Phone is a staff/manual contact task or call-script surface unless a later card defines AI-generated phone copy. WhatsApp is out of scope until typed consent/provider/audit semantics exist.
- Channel availability is not consent. Every send candidate needs channel, destination reference, consent/opt-out status, quiet-hours/location policy, delivery suppression/failure state, approval/send path, and audit/idempotency metadata.
- Default send mode is `draft_for_review` unless the category explicitly says a later deterministic path may be considered. A draft is not a send.
- Customer-facing sends are approval-gated until product policy approves a deterministic path for a narrow class with fixed template, facts, recipient, channel, idempotency, audit, and suppression behavior.
- Medical, vaccine, medication, allergy, behavior, incident, safety, payment, refund, waiver, cancellation, overbooking, waitlist, rejection, policy-exception, and booking-promise facts require staff/manager review before customer-facing copy.
- Customer copy must be warm, concise, factual, pet-parent friendly, and source-grounded. Do not invent prices, dates, availability, health interpretation, refundability, deadlines, service eligibility, or cheerful care details.

## Category caveats to preserve

- `inquiry`, `missing info`, and `pre-arrival` may look low-risk, but they still must not smuggle in availability, eligibility, vaccine, payment, or policy commitments.
- `booking offer/confirmation`, `deposit request`, and `waitlist` are customer-visible operational commitments. They require trusted source facts and human approval before promises, deadlines, capacity status, payment links, or policy consequences are sent.
- `vaccine reminder` is a customer-message category, not a vaccine approval workflow. Upload/extraction/review states must remain distinct from accepted medical eligibility.
- `daily update` should suppress or route to review when an incident, health/medication exception, behavior concern, photo/privacy issue, or missing evidence would make routine upbeat copy misleading.
- `incident draft` is always review-gated. The agent may prepare owner-facing copy for a reviewer, but cannot notify owners, downgrade severity, close incidents, clear restrictions, or provide legal/medical conclusions.
- `checkout` and `review request` must check unresolved incidents, care tasks, payment issues, complaints, and suppression rules before any customer-facing follow-up.

## Minimum category output fields for implementation

Each generated category recommendation should carry:

- `category`: one of the taxonomy names above.
- `trigger_event`: semantic workflow event, provider boundary event, or staff task source.
- `subject_refs`: location, customer, pet(s), reservation/stay, payment/document/incident/draft ids as applicable.
- `recipient`: customer/owner, payer, staff/front desk, manager, medical reviewer, or internal review queue.
- `channel_candidates`: allowed channel candidates with consent/opt-out/quiet-hours/suppression state.
- `canonical_facts`: normalized facts and evidence refs used by the template.
- `missing_or_blocking_facts`: explicit gaps, stale facts, conflicts, or sensitivity flags.
- `send_mode`: `draft_for_review`, `internal_review_packet`, `staff_call_task`, `deterministic_send_candidate`, or `suppressed`.
- `required_approvals`: review gates and approver roles needed before send or side effect.
- `template_key`: stable template/category key plus version when an approved template exists; otherwise a source-derived candidate key.
- `idempotency_key`: semantic key based on category, subject, event/evidence, template version, and policy version; never raw message text.
- `audit_refs`: source event ids, policy snapshot ids, approval ids, prior draft/send refs, and provider/outbox refs when applicable.
